# User Journey

## Purpose

This document describes the ideal experience of using ParMind from the moment a golfer arrives at the course until they leave with a clear understanding of how to improve.

The journey focuses on reducing cognitive load while reinforcing better decision-making throughout the round.

---

# Phase 1 — Arrive

## Goal

Prepare the golfer mentally before the first tee.

### User Mindset

- Excited
- Slightly nervous
- Thinking about today's score
- Wants confidence, not complexity

### ParMind's Responsibility

- Open instantly.
- Resume unfinished rounds automatically if needed.
- Make starting a new round take less than 15 seconds.
- Avoid unnecessary setup.
- Reinforce process instead of score.

### Success Criteria

The golfer reaches the first tee feeling prepared rather than distracted.

---

# Phase 2 — Start Round

## Goal

Begin tracking the round with minimal effort.

### User Actions

- Tap "Start Round."
- Select course (or choose a quick practice round if no course is selected).
- Confirm number of holes.
- Begin Hole 1.

### ParMind's Responsibility

The app should never ask for information that is not immediately valuable.

Starting a round should feel effortless.

### Success Criteria

The golfer reaches Hole 1 with almost no friction.

---

# Phase 3 — Play the Round

## Goal

Help the golfer reflect without interrupting play.

### After Each Hole

The golfer records:

- Score
- Putts
- Penalties
- Decision quality
- Commitment
- Emotional control
- Optional note

### Design Principles

- One-handed interaction.
- Large touch targets.
- Minimal typing.
- Complete a hole in under 20 seconds.

### Success Criteria

Recording a hole feels like a natural pause between holes rather than an administrative task.

---

# Phase 4 — Finish Round

## Goal

Transition from recording to learning.

### User Actions

- Finish Hole 18.
- View Round Summary.
- Complete a brief reflection.

### Reflection Questions

- What was my best decision today?
- What decision cost me the most?
- What will I commit to next round?

### Success Criteria

The golfer leaves with one clear lesson instead of dozens of statistics.

---

# Phase 5 — Learn

## Goal

Turn one round into long-term improvement.

### User Experience

The golfer reviews:

- Decision score
- Mental game trends
- Round history
- Notes
- Personal commitments

The emphasis is on identifying patterns rather than judging individual rounds.

### Success Criteria

The golfer understands why the round unfolded the way it did and has a clear focus for the next round.

---

# Experience Principles

Every phase of the journey should satisfy the following:

- The app reduces cognitive load.
- The next action is always obvious.
- The golfer spends more time thinking about golf than about the app.
- Reflection is constructive rather than judgmental.
- Every interaction reinforces better decision-making.

---

# Definition of Success

The ideal user closes ParMind after every round thinking:

> "I know exactly what I need to improve before my next round."
