# Advantage Zone

## Purpose

The Advantage Zone is ParMind's core strategic concept.

It changes the golfer's objective from chasing perfect shots to creating the highest-probability opportunity for the next shot.

The Advantage Zone is not simply a distance.

It is a position on the golf course from which the golfer has the highest probability of making a confident, disciplined decision.

---

# Philosophy

Traditional golf teaches players to chase Greens in Regulation.

ParMind teaches players to create and maintain advantage.

After every shot, the question becomes:

> "Did this shot improve my advantage?"

This simple question encourages disciplined course management and reduces the tendency to compound mistakes.

---

# Examples

## Recovery

A drive finishes behind trees.

Instead of attempting a low-percentage hero shot, the golfer plays safely back into their Advantage Zone.

The objective changes from saving par immediately to restoring control of the hole.

---

## Strategy

On a reachable par 5, the golfer lays up to their preferred yardage instead of forcing a risky second shot.

The golfer intentionally creates an Advantage Zone opportunity.

---

# Personalization

Every golfer has a different Advantage Zone.

For one player it may be 120 yards.

For another it may be 85 yards.

ParMind helps golfers discover and refine their Advantage Zone over time using their own performance data.

---

# Product Principle

A successful shot is not always the one that finishes closest to the hole.

A successful shot is one that improves the golfer's advantage.

---

# Success

Golfers should eventually begin thinking this way without opening the app.

The ultimate goal of ParMind is to permanently improve the golfer's decision-making process.
